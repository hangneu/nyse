<B>
	Introductory Note
</B>
</P>
<P STYLE="MARGIN-TOP:12PT; MARGIN-BOTTOM:0PT; FONT-SIZE:10PT; FONT-FAMILY:TIMES NEW ROMAN">
	Lehman ABS Corporation (the &#147;Depositor&#148; or the &#147;Registrant&#148;) is the Depositor in respect of the Corporate Backed Trust Certificates,
	Goldman Sachs Capital I Securities-Backed Series 2004-6 Trust (the &#147;Trust&#148;), a common law trust formed pursuant to the Standard Terms for Trust Agreements, dated as of January&nbsp;16, 2001, between the Depositor and U.S. Bank Trust
	National Association, as trustee (the &#147;Trustee&#148;), as supplemented by a Series Supplement (the &#147;Series Supplement&#148;) dated as of March&nbsp;19, 2004 in respect of the Trust. The Trust&#146;s assets consist solely of capital
	Securities issued by Goldman Sachs Capital I (the &#147;Underlying Securities Issuer&#148;). The Certificates do not represent obligations of, or interests in, the Depositor or the Trustee.
</P>
<P STYLE="MARGIN-TOP:12PT; MARGIN-BOTTOM:0PT; FONT-SIZE:10PT; FONT-FAMILY:TIMES NEW ROMAN">
	The Depositor is a wholly-owned, indirect subsidiary of Lehman Brothers Holdings Inc. (&#147;LBHI&#148;).&nbsp;On September&nbsp;15, 2008 and periodically
	thereafter, LBHI and certain of its subsidiaries (collectively, the &#147;Debtors&#148;) commenced voluntary cases under chapter 11 of title 11 of the United States Code with the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of New York
	(the &#147;Bankruptcy Court&#148;) (jointly administered proceedings,
<I>
	In re Lehman Brothers Holdings Inc.
</I>
	et al., Case Number 08-13555). LBHI and Lehman Brothers Inc., have sold since September&nbsp;15, 2008 significant businesses, including
	the sale on September&nbsp;21, 2008 of the investment banking business to Barclays Capital Inc., which business included the employees who historically conducted the Registrant&#146;s business.
</P>
<P STYLE="MARGIN-TOP:12PT; MARGIN-BOTTOM:0PT; FONT-SIZE:10PT; FONT-FAMILY:TIMES NEW ROMAN">
	On December&nbsp;6, 2011, the Bankruptcy Court entered an order (the &#147;Confirmation Order&#148;) confirming the Modified Third Amended Joint Chapter 11
	Plan of Lehman Brothers Holdings Inc. and its Affiliated Debtors (the &#147;Plan&#148;).&nbsp;The Plan and the Confirmation Order were attached as Exhibit 2.1 and Exhibit 2.2, respectively, to LBHI&#146;s Current Report on Form 8-K filed with the
	Securities and Exchange Commission on December&nbsp;14, 2011.&nbsp;LBHI&#146;s Current Report on Form 8-K filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission on December&nbsp;14, 2011 contains a summary of the Plan.
</P>
<P STYLE="MARGIN-TOP:12PT; MARGIN-BOTTOM:0PT; FONT-SIZE:10PT; FONT-FAMILY:TIMES NEW ROMAN">
	On March&nbsp;6, 2012 (the &#147;Effective Date&#148;), the Plan became effective and a notice of the Effective Date of the Plan (the &#147;Notice of
	Effective Date&#148;) was filed with the Bankruptcy Court.
</P>
<P STYLE="MARGIN-TOP:12PT; MARGIN-BOTTOM:0PT; FONT-SIZE:10PT; FONT-FAMILY:TIMES NEW ROMAN">
	On November&nbsp;30, 2015, the Trustee sent a notice (the &#147;Notice&#148;) to holders of
	Trust Certificates stating that LBHI, as Plan administrator on behalf of Lehman Brothers Special Financing Inc. (&#147;LBSF&#148;), initiated a mediation proceeding with the Trust pursuant to an order of the Bankruptcy Court. The mediation is
	related to a complaint filed by LBSF against the Trust on September&nbsp;18, 2015 arising out of a swap transaction between LBSF and the Trust (the &#147;Swap Transaction&#148;). The Notice states that the complaint &#147;seeks a determination,
	among other things, that LBSF is entitled to a Termination Payment [as defined in the Series Supplement] with respect to any Termination of the Swap Transaction, or alternatively that it is entitled to the Fixed Payments it alleges are due by the
	Trust.&#148;&nbsp;In the event that an Early Termination Payment or judgment is payable by the Trust to LBSF, the payment may need to be satisfied by the distribution to LBSF of a portion of the underlying securities or the proceeds thereof.
</P>
<P STYLE="MARGIN-TOP:12PT; MARGIN-BOTTOM:0PT; FONT-SIZE:10PT; FONT-FAMILY:TIMES NEW ROMAN">
	Additionally, the Notice sets forth that prior to any distribution to holders of Trust Certificates, funds held under the Trust Agreement may be used first
	for payment of the fees and costs incurred or to be incurred by the Trustee in performing its duties, which include compensation for time spent, and the fees and costs of counsel and other agents employed, to pursue remedies, defend claims, or take
	other actions to protect the interests of the holders of Trust Certificates and the Trust, respectively. A copy of the Notice was attached as Exhibit 99.2 to the Current Report on Form 8-K filed by the Registrant on February 26, 2016. The Trustee
	has advised the Depositor that it intends to file a Trust Instruction Proceeding in Ramsey County, Minnesota, seeking &#147;an order of the Court authorizing and directing the Trustee to use trust property to pay extraordinary fees and expenses that
	have been incurred by the Trustee in connection with the LBHI bankruptcy proceeding to date and hereafter, including, but not limited to the fees and costs of administering and defending the Litigation.&#148; Please refer to Sections 5
	(&#147;Distributions&#148;) and 8 (&#147;Trustee&#146;s Fees&#148;) of the Series Supplement for further information regarding expenses.
</P>
